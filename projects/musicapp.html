Play your mood!

Music Player App UI Project

Overview

I’ve realised that there in no music app where you can choose the music based on your mood. In others music players it isn’t that easy and simple. The problem was also the level of interaction with other users.

Approach

The app is divided into four different moods distinguished by different styles appropriate to the mood of the user. In every sections you can find different playlists and you can also create and share your own one.

User Research

I focused on spotify as a champion of music app players. According to Spotify, 72% of Spotify listeners are millennials, with 26% 18–24-year-olds, 29% 25–34-year-olds, 16% 35–44-year-olds, 11% 45–54-year-olds, 19% 55 and older. Millennials represent the largest user demographic.

I’ve made a survey and I’ve sent it to my friends aged 18-24 (20 persons) to ask about their preferences and difficulties in using the mobile music players and to get to know their profile.

CONCLUSIONS

- 80% of tested users are listening to the music more than 6 hours a day
- the users are mostly listening to the music on the way (car, public transport)
- no one user can say whether he/she is listening to more happy or more sad music
- the users usually play music from theirplaylist
- 50% of the users think it is hard to find new songs and artists
- most of the users find new artists on the recommendation of friends (at a party, a friend shares a song)
- 80% of tested users are using Spotify, 20% are using Apple Music
- Spotify's featured playlist works not good, not allowing to explore new songs
- 90% of tested users think they listen to all kinds of music
